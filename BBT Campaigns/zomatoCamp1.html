Expert Analysis

The BBT Verdict

🎯
Campaign objective determines campaign rating.
Zomato's campaign is outstanding — for awareness. Asian Paints' campaign is outstanding — for conversion. Rating improves when every element supports the defined business objective.
🔴
Awareness without a funnel is incomplete marketing.
Zomato's sustainability campaign built brand equity but left measurable ROI on the table. Without a conversion path, even brilliant campaigns score lower on business impact.
🟢
Asian Paints won because it combined branding and commerce.
The "Kaam Hamara" campaign had strong brand communication AND a clear service offer AND a measurable funnel. That triple combination is what elevates a campaign from good to great.
Strategic Summary

What brands should learn from this.

The most important lesson from both campaigns is that successful campaigns are not built only on ads. They are built on clarity of objective. If the objective is awareness and trust, Zomato's model is highly effective. If the objective is leads, service sales, and measurable conversions, Asian Paints offers a stronger blueprint.

For founders and brands, the key question is always: What is the business objective behind this campaign? Ratings improve when every element — message, channel, creative, funnel — supports that single objective.

Neither campaign is wrong. They serve different purposes. The best marketers know which type of campaign they need and when — and build accordingly.

Key Takeaways
  • Always define the campaign objective before creating any creative
  • Awareness campaigns need different metrics than conversion campaigns
  • A strong funnel system separates a 7.7 campaign from an 8.8 campaign
  • Sustainability messaging works for trust but needs a conversion layer
  • Pain-point marketing combined with a clear offer drives the highest ROI
  • Great branding + clear funnel = category leadership
